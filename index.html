Bird Strike on engine blades using LS-Dyna.

A classic non-linear transient dynamics problem, the bird strike problem was solved using a generic explicit solver in LS-Dyna.

Oct 2020 - Dec 2020 Skill-lync
(click for
project report)

▪ While accurate modeling of the problems requires advanced techniques such as Smoothed Particle Hydrodynamics, the aim here was to understand the nuances such as consistent numbering approach, contact-modeling, control card setup, and usage of multiple input files akin to industry model setup in LS-PrePost.
▪ The model consisted of 4 components: blades, hub, bird, and casing.
▪ General Single surface, node-to-surface, and surface-to-surface contacts have been used. A mass-Scaling of 3.2% has been used to study its impact on the simulation time.

  • LS-Dyna | Skill-Lync

Natural Convection: Fluid Flow using FEniCS.

The underlying mechanism for this simulation involves fluid flow coupled to thermal energy transport through temperature-dependent buoyant forces.

Jan 2020 - May 2020 UC-Boulder
(click for
project details)

▪ The idea of this numerical study is to model convection coupling the Navier-Stokes Equations and Advection-Diffusion Equation.
▪ The first phase of the project was to validate the solution of equations by simulating a strady recirculating flow in a square cavity.
▪ The main study focused on a practical engineering application: analyzing the natural convective flow around a heated plate geometry.
▪ The final problem was implementing Unsteady Convection in a Differential Heated Cavity using Monolithic Solve solution algorithm.

  • FEniCS - A Python based PDE Solver

Head Impact Simulation using LS-Dyna.

This project on Pedestrian Head Impact simulation was a part of the CAE master’s certification at Skill-Lync. Part of the focus was to understand a simple explicit analysis setup for vehicle safety engineering.

Oct 2020 - Dec 2020 Skill-Lync
(click for
project report)

▪ In this sample, a child head foam model was used for the impact of the head foam on a car bonnet.
▪ A piecewise linear plasticity material model was considered for the bonnet, whereas the head foam model has different parts modeled with different materials.
▪ The skull, backplate, and accelerator block have been modeled with a rigid material and the skin was modeled with rubber (MAT_77 ODGEN).
▪ The final problem was to study the injury caused to the head by calculating Head Injury Coefficient (HIC).

Re-design of a Surveyor’s Wheel - Design for Manufacturability.

▪ Through the reverse engineering phase, work done made use of DFM, and DFA principles to analyze a consumer product in significant depth to identify areas of improvement and increase its functionality.
▪ The re-design of the product inculcated changes such as minute alteration of materials, the inclusion of a paint spraying wand (to allow for marking of the area measured), and DFA improvements (decrease of total part count).
